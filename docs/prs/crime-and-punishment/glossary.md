## Definitions

### Crime

An action that breaks the law.

### Punishment

Something bad done to somebody as a consequence, usually of breaking the law, like imprisonment.

### Corporal punishment

:::warning

Don't confuse corporal punishment with [capital punishment](#death-penalty)! They are two different things.

:::

The act of physical pain/suffering being inflicted on somebody as [punishment](#punishment).

<span id="capital-punishment"></span>
### Death penalty

A form of [corporal punishment](#corporal-punishment) where a person is killed for their actions. In the UK, it was [abolished for murder in 1965](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1965/71) and [abolished for treason in 1998](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1998/37/section/36).

*Synonyms: capital punishment*

Main document: [Capital Punishment](./capital-punishment.md)

### Hate crime

A [crime](#crime) based on prejudice held by the criminal.

### Murder

The act of unlawfully killing somebody. Murder is a [crime](#crime).

### Theft

The act of taking something without the owner's consent. Theft is a [crime](#crime).

### Poverty

Being without easy access to basic needs like food, water or housing. Poverty is a [cause of crime](./causes-of-crime.md).

### Mental illness

A medical condition that affects a person's mood or emotions. Mental illness is a [cause of crime](./causes-of-crime.md).

### Addiction

Dependence on a substance or activity that is very hard to overcome. Addiction is a [cause of crime](./causes-of-crime.md).

### Greed

Wanting to possess excessive things that are not needed, e.g. wealth. Greed is a [cause of crime](./causes-of-crime.md) and [reason for war](../peace-and-conflict/reasons-for-war.md).

### Evil

The opposite of good. In religion, it is usually used to mean against God or religious teachings.  
Christians call evil acts sins.  
Buddhists call evil acts unskillful (and good acts skillful).
